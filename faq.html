Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about residency in Turkey. Get instant answers to common questions.

🏘️ Residency Questions

What are the main types of residency permits in Turkey? +

Turkey offers several residency options including:

  • Tourist Residency (30-90 days)
  • Student Residency (renewable annually)
  • Real Estate Residency (€250,000+ investment)
  • Family Sponsorship
  • Work Visa
How long does the residency application process take? +

Processing times vary by residency type:

  • Tourist Visa: On arrival (Schengen-free entry for 90 days)
  • Long-term Residency: 15-30 days for document processing
  • Real Estate Residency: 5-7 business days

Our service typically expedites this within 10-15 business days.

What documents do I need for residency application? +

Basic requirements typically include:

  • Valid passport (minimum 6 months validity)
  • Proof of accommodation (rental contract or property deed)
  • Proof of income or financial means
  • Health insurance coverage
  • Criminal background certificate
  • Completed application form

We provide a complete checklist customized to your specific situation.

Can I work while on residency permit? +

Work permissions depend on your residency type:

  • Tourist Residency: Work permission required separately
  • Student Residency: Limited work hours allowed (20 hours/week)
  • Real Estate Residency: Can establish business or become freelancer
  • Work Visa: Full employment rights with employer sponsorship

We can guide you through obtaining work authorization alongside residency.

What is the cost of getting Turkish residency? +

Costs vary by residency type:

  • Tourist Residency: Free (automatic for 90 days)
  • Short-term Residency: ₺1,200-2,000 per year
  • Real Estate Investment: €250,000+ property purchase
  • Service Fees: Our professional assistance typically ranges €500-1,500 depending on complexity

Get a free consultation for exact pricing based on your situation.

💼 Work & Companies

Can I start a business in Turkey on residency? +

Yes! Many residency types allow business ownership:

  • Register as a sole proprietor or establish a limited company
  • Work visa holders can work for registered company
  • Real estate visa holders can own and manage businesses
  • Freelancers can operate as independent professionals

We assist with company registration and work permits.

What is required to sponsor an employee in Turkey? +

As an employer, you'll need to:

  • Have a registered Turkish company
  • Demonstrate financial stability
  • Provide employment contract
  • Pay mandatory social security contributions
  • Apply through Ministry of Labor and Social Security

Processing typically takes 2-4 weeks.

What are the taxation requirements for residents? +

Taxation depends on your residency status:

  • Limited Residents: Tax on Turkish income only
  • Unlimited Residents: Global income subject to taxation
  • Residents can benefit from tax treaties if applicable
  • Annual tax filing required with Turkish authorities

We recommend consulting with a tax professional for personalized advice.

🛡️ Insurance & Other Topics

Is health insurance mandatory for residency? +

Yes, health insurance is required:

  • Turkish Social Security Insurance (SGK) is mandatory for working residents
  • Private insurance acceptable for non-working residents
  • Minimum coverage must include emergency care
  • Annual cost approximately ₺1,200-3,000

We help arrange appropriate insurance coverage.

Can I buy property as a foreign resident? +

Yes, with some restrictions:

  • Foreign nationals can purchase property with proof of funds
  • Property purchase can lead to residency qualification
  • Some regions have restrictions on land (agricultural areas)
  • Tax clearance required from country of origin
  • Total property value no more than 30% of Ankara value for security

We connect you with trusted real estate partners.

What happens if my residency expires? +

Residency renewal is straightforward:

  • Most residencies are renewable annually
  • Apply 30-60 days before expiration
  • Requirements similar to initial application
  • Processing typically faster for renewals
  • Limited tourists can leave and re-enter for another 90 days

We send renewal reminders and handle all paperwork.

Can family members get residency based on my permit? +

Dependent family can join you:

  • Spouse and children can apply as dependents
  • Additional documentation proving relationship required
  • Financial support proof for dependents needed
  • Children gain automatic dependency rights

Family reunion visas have special expedited processing.

Is Turkish citizenship possible from residency? +

Yes, citizenship is possible through residency:

  • Continuous residency of 5+ years required
  • Language requirement (basic Turkish)
  • Character background check passed
  • Property investment of €250K+ qualifies for 3-year path
  • No renunciation of current nationality required

Dual citizenship is permitted under Turkish law.

💰 Residency Tax by Nationality

How much is the residency tax in Turkey? +

How much does a residence permit in Turkey cost? Residence permit fees (ikamet harcı) are based on the principle of reciprocity, so the residency fee depends on your nationality and on the type of short-term residence permit — tourist, real estate, family or medical. The fees below are valid from 01/01/2026, as determined by the General Communiqué on the Fees Law published in the Official Gazette No. 33124 dated 31 December 2025 by the Presidency of Migration Management (Göç İdaresi).

Residence card fee — applies to all nationalities: For every nationality, a residence card fee of 964 TL is added to the residence permit application fee. Nationalities that are exempt from the residency fee pay only this 964 TL residence card fee.
Standard residence permit fee — first month 25 USD · each additional month 5 USD
This standard residency fee applies to most nationalities, including the citizens of any country not listed in the special reciprocity groups below.

Reduced residence permit fees for specific nationalities:

First month 14 USD · each additional month 3.5 USD
Albania, Cambodia, Israel, Japan, Mali, North Macedonia, Oman, Philippines, Tajikistan
First month 9 USD · each additional month 2.5 USD
Algeria, Belarus, Belgium, Jordan, Kuwait, Luxembourg, Malta, Singapore, Spain, Taiwan
First month 7 USD · each additional month 1.5 USD
Ethiopia, Hong Kong, Micronesia, Russia, South Korea
First month 5 USD · each additional month 0.5 USD
Côte d'Ivoire, Egypt, Iraq, Montenegro, Morocco, Tunisia

Residency fee exemption: student residence permits and humanitarian residence permits — as well as certain nationalities — are exempt from the residence permit fee in Turkey; in that case only the 964 TL residence card fee is paid.
